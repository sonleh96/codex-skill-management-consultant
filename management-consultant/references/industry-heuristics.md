## Universal Heuristics

These apply across almost every industry:

**Follow the money**: Map where value is created and where it pools. In any value chain, some stages capture disproportionate profit. Understand why.

**Revenue = Price × Volume**: This decomposition works everywhere. If revenue is the problem, figure out if it's a price problem or a volume problem. They have very different solutions.

**Fixed cost leverage**: Businesses with high fixed costs and low variable costs (software, airlines, hotels) have enormous operating leverage — small revenue changes create large profit swings.

**Switching costs drive moats**: The harder it is for customers to leave, the more pricing power and stability the business has. Assess switching costs in every industry.

**Regulation creates both barriers and opportunities**: Regulation limits competition (good for incumbents) but also creates compliance costs and rigidity. Changes in regulation are often the biggest disruptive events.

**Cyclicality**: Understand where the industry is in its cycle. Decisions appropriate at the peak (capacity expansion) can be disastrous at the trough.

**Industry concentration**: Fragmented industries (many small players) behave very differently from consolidated ones (few large players). Consolidation usually follows a predictable S-curve.

## Technology & Software

### Key Metrics
- **ARR** (Annual Recurring Revenue): The north star for SaaS
- **Net Revenue Retention (NRR)**: >110% is good, >130% is elite (expansion exceeds churn)
- **Gross Margin**: 70-85% for SaaS, 50-65% for hardware
- **Rule of 40**: Revenue growth % + EBITDA margin % should exceed 40%
- **Magic Number**: Net new ARR / Prior quarter S&M spend. >0.75 is efficient.
- **CAC Payback**: Months to recoup acquisition cost. <18 months is healthy.
- **LTV:CAC**: >3:1 is the benchmark

### Key Dynamics
- **Land and expand**: Win small, grow big within accounts
- **Network effects**: Value increases with users (marketplaces, social, collaboration tools)
- **Winner-take-most**: In categories with strong network effects, #1 captures most of the value
- **Freemium economics**: Free tier as top-of-funnel; conversion rate of 2-5% to paid is typical
- **Platform vs. application**: Platforms (AWS, Salesforce) have stronger moats than point solutions
- **AI disruption**: Rapidly changing cost structures and competitive dynamics in 2024+

### Valuation
- High-growth SaaS: 10-20x ARR (higher for >40% growth)
- Mature SaaS: 5-10x ARR
- Hardware/services: 1-4x revenue
- EV/EBITDA for profitable tech: 15-30x

## Financial Services

### Key Metrics (by sub-sector)
**Banking**: Net Interest Margin (NIM), Cost-to-Income ratio, Loan-to-Deposit ratio, NPL ratio, ROE, CET1 capital ratio
**Insurance**: Combined ratio (<100% = underwriting profit), Loss ratio, Expense ratio, Investment yield
**Asset Management**: AUM, Fee rate (bps), Cost-to-income, Net flows
**Fintech/Payments**: Take rate, Payment volume, Revenue per user, Transaction cost

### Key Dynamics
- **Interest rate sensitivity**: Banks' NIM expands/compresses with rate changes
- **Regulatory capital**: Banks must hold capital against risk-weighted assets — constrains leverage and growth
- **Credit cycle**: Loan losses are cyclical — provisions spike in downturns
- **Fee compression**: Passive investing, robo-advisors, and competition are compressing fees
- **Embedded finance**: Financial services unbundling into non-financial products (BNPL, insurance at checkout)
- **Compliance cost**: 10-15% of revenue in large banks goes to compliance/risk management

## Healthcare & Life Sciences

### Key Metrics
**Pharma**: R&D spend as % of revenue (15-25%), Pipeline value, Patent cliffs, Market share by therapeutic area
**Hospitals/Providers**: Bed occupancy rate, Case mix index, Revenue per adjusted discharge, Operating margin (2-5% typical)
**Payers (Insurance)**: Medical loss ratio (MLR), Administrative cost ratio, Member months, Per-member-per-month (PMPM) cost
**Med Devices**: Installed base, Razor/blade ratio, Procedure volumes

### Key Dynamics
- **Patent cliff**: When blockbuster drugs lose patent protection, generic competition destroys pricing (80-90% price decline)
- **Payer mix matters enormously**: Medicare/Medicaid reimburse less than commercial insurance
- **Regulatory pathway**: FDA approval timelines (8-12 years from discovery to market, $1-2B cost)
- **Value-based care shift**: Moving from fee-for-service (volume) to value-based (outcomes)
- **Aging demographics**: Structural demand growth across healthcare
- **Consolidation**: Both payers and providers have consolidated significantly

## Consumer & Retail

### Key Metrics
- **Same-store sales growth (SSS)**: Organic growth metric (excludes new stores)
- **Revenue per square foot**: Productivity metric for physical retail
- **Gross margin**: Varies widely (grocery 25-30%, apparel 50-65%, luxury 65-75%)
- **Inventory turns**: How many times inventory sells per year (grocery 15-20x, apparel 4-8x)
- **Customer acquisition cost (CAC)** and **Customer lifetime value (LTV)**
- **Basket size** and **visit frequency**
- **Conversion rate**: % of visitors who buy (2-3% online, 20-40% in-store)

### Key Dynamics
- **Omnichannel is table stakes**: Customers expect seamless online-offline experience
- **Private label growth**: Retailers' own brands growing share (higher margin for retailer)
- **Last-mile economics**: Delivery cost is the Achilles' heel of e-commerce grocery
- **Brand vs. performance marketing**: Shift toward measurable, performance-driven spend
- **Consumer bifurcation**: Premium and value growing; middle squeezed
- **Seasonal patterns**: Retail is heavily seasonal (Q4 is 30-40% of annual revenue for many retailers)

## Industrial & Manufacturing

### Key Metrics
- **OEE (Overall Equipment Effectiveness)**: Availability × Performance × Quality. World-class is >85%.
- **Capacity utilization**: % of maximum output being used
- **Scrap/waste rate**: % of input that doesn't become saleable output
- **On-time delivery**: % of orders delivered when promised
- **Inventory days**: Days of inventory on hand (raw, WIP, finished goods separately)
- **EBITDA margin**: 10-15% typical for industrial, 15-25% for specialty/high-value

### Key Dynamics
- **Capital intensity**: High fixed costs create operating leverage and barrier to entry
- **Cycle sensitivity**: Highly correlated to GDP and industrial production
- **Aftermarket/services**: Often 30-50% of profit from installed base services (higher margin than equipment)
- **Supply chain complexity**: Multi-tier supply chains with long lead times
- **Lean/continuous improvement**: Mature companies have well-developed production systems
- **Industry 4.0**: IoT, predictive maintenance, digital twins transforming operations
- **ESG/sustainability**: Increasingly important for industrial companies (emissions, waste, circular economy)

## Energy & Utilities

### Key Metrics
- **LCOE (Levelized Cost of Energy)**: Cost per MWh over the life of a generation asset
- **Reserve replacement ratio**: Ratio of new reserves found to production (for upstream O&G)
- **Capacity factor**: Actual output / maximum possible output (wind ~35%, solar ~25%, nuclear ~90%)
- **Rate base**: The asset base on which regulated utilities earn a return
- **ROE (regulated)**: Typically 9-11% for US regulated utilities

### Key Dynamics
- **Energy transition**: Shift from fossil fuels to renewables is the defining megatrend
- **Regulated vs. unregulated**: Regulated utilities have guaranteed returns but limited growth; unregulated have higher risk/reward
- **Commodity exposure**: Upstream E&P companies' profitability is driven by oil/gas prices
- **Capital intensity**: Energy is one of the most capital-intensive sectors
- **Policy dependency**: Subsidies, carbon pricing, and regulations shape investment decisions
- **Storage**: Battery economics rapidly improving, enabling intermittent renewables

## Telecom & Media

### Key Metrics
- **ARPU (Average Revenue Per User)**: Key revenue metric
- **Churn rate**: Monthly subscriber losses (1-2% monthly is typical for wireless)
- **Capex intensity**: Capex as % of revenue (telecom: 15-20%)
- **Subscribers / market penetration**: Total and growth rate
- **Content cost per subscriber** (media/streaming)
- **LTV:SAC** (Subscriber Acquisition Cost)

### Key Dynamics
- **Infrastructure is a natural monopoly**: High fixed costs favor scale; last-mile is the moat
- **Bundling**: TV + internet + mobile drives retention and ARPU
- **OTT disruption**: Streaming services disrupted traditional TV economics
- **5G investment cycle**: Massive capex for uncertain revenue upside
- **Cord-cutting**: Linear TV subscribers declining 5-8% annually
- **Content arms race**: Streaming services spending billions on original content with unclear unit economics

## Private Equity / Investor Lens

When working with PE clients, evaluate businesses differently:

### Key Metrics
- **EBITDA and EBITDA margin**: The fundamental valuation metric for PE
- **Free cash flow conversion**: EBITDA → FCF bridge (capex, working capital, tax)
- **Revenue quality**: Recurring vs. one-time, contractual vs. at-will, concentration
- **Customer concentration**: Top 10 customers as % of revenue (>25% is a risk)
- **Management quality**: Depth of bench, track record, retention
- **Entry multiple**: EV/EBITDA at purchase (typically 8-15x)
- **Target returns**: 20-25% IRR, 2.5-3.5x MOIC over 3-5 years

### Value Creation Levers (the PE playbook)
1. **Revenue growth**: Organic + acquisitions (buy-and-build)
2. **Margin expansion**: Operational efficiency, pricing, procurement
3. **Multiple expansion**: Enter at 8x, exit at 11x (through growth, quality improvements)
4. **Financial engineering**: Debt leverage (amplifies equity returns)
5. **Add-on acquisitions**: Buy smaller competitors at lower multiples

## Public Sector

### Key Dynamics
- **Budget cycles**: Annual appropriations, multi-year funding streams
- **Procurement**: Competitive bidding, RFP processes, long sales cycles
- **Stakeholder complexity**: Elected officials, career civil servants, citizens, unions
- **Impact measurement**: Outcomes-based metrics replacing output-based metrics
- **Regulation as product**: Government's "product" is often regulation, policy, and services
- **IT modernization**: Large legacy systems, security requirements, slow procurement = large consulting opportunity
- **Workforce challenges**: Aging workforce, difficulty competing with private sector on compensation

### Key Metrics
- **Cost per outcome** (cost per student, cost per patient, cost per case resolved)
- **Service levels** (wait times, case backlogs, processing times)
- **Citizen satisfaction** (surveys, NPS equivalents)
- **Budget variance** (actual vs. planned spend)
