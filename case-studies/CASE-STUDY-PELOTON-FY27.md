# Case Study: Peloton FY27

## Introduction
This case study explores Peloton's strategic journey and examines the key consulting frameworks essential for understanding profitability, market positioning, capital allocation, and strategic turnaround. We employ the SCQA methodology, issue trees, and scenario modeling to provide a structured analysis.

## SCQA Methodology
- **Situation**: Peloton's current market landscape post-COVID-19, including shifts in consumer behavior and competition.
- **Complication**: Declining subscription growth and the impact on profitability.
- **Question**: How can Peloton enhance its profitability while maintaining market leadership?
- **Answer**: Implementing strategies for market diversification, optimizing capital allocation, and rebranding efforts.

## Profitability Analysis
- Detailed analysis of revenue streams vs. costs.
- Evaluation of subscription vs. product sales margins.
- Assessment of operational efficiencies and cost-cutting measures.

## Market Positioning
- Evaluation of Peloton’s brand identity and market perception.
- Competitive analysis of key players in the fitness industry.
- Recommendations for strengthening market position.

## Capital Allocation
- Analysis of current capital allocation effectiveness.
- Recommendations for reallocating resources to high-impact areas.
- Assessment of investment in technology and product innovation.

## Strategic Turnaround
- Identification of key challenges facing Peloton.
- Development of turnaround strategies including marketing, partnerships, and product offerings.
- Scenario modeling to project outcomes based on strategic decisions.

## Conclusion
This structured case approach offers insights into the strategic pathways Peloton can pursue to ensure sustainable growth and profitability for FY27 and beyond.
